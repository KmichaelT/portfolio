AyePlus

Ayeplus is course management app that aimed to allow schools to automate the learning and teaching process. I worked on this project on the couse of 2 weeks. below, i show case the general design process.

The problem

Schools in low income communities, especially in developing countries school systems still run in paper. The old paper based system is time and money consuming and complicated.

The goal

Design an app that will improve the education system in low income communities by allowing students and teachers run courses easily and efficiently.

My role

UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery

Responsibilities:

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

Research

The initial stage in the process is to research how schools perform their learning and teaching processes, identify common tasks, and determine how we can make them more efficient with our application.

Sketching

After conducting research to determine the most typical school activities, I sketched a rough layout of the general user interface.

Low-fidelity prototype

To prepare for usability testing, I created a low-fidelity prototype that connected the user flow of logging in, switching between courses, accessing assignments and messaging.

High-fidelity prototype

The high-fidelity prototype followed the same user flow as the low-fidelity prototype, including design changes made after the usability study.
